<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"></p> <h3>Final Project: Robot Boxing</h3> I started with 4 servo motors inside of a body similar to the final body, however it was smaller and I had to iterate a bit to get the holes to be the correct size and put some guard rails to keep the servos in place. This was my MVP and I was able to plug it in and have all the servos rotate back and forth. It was suggested that I switch the wheels to stepper motors. I did and had many problems with them. I had them moving many times but I think the issue was that I was never able to supply enough power and/or the XIAO did not have enough sophisticated pins to properly run 2 four-pin stepper motors and a servo. I discarded an arm because there were not enough pins on the XIAO. --- ### Issues & How I Fixed Them (with help from Claude) **1. Output speed range was too wide** The potToSpeed() function was originally mapping joystick inputs to a range of -100 to 100, which caused the wheels to spin too fast and made the robot hard to control. I used Claude to update the map() calls to output -15 to 15 instead, giving much finer control at lower speeds. **2. Wrong robot MAC address** ESP-NOW requires the exact MAC address of the receiving device. My code had a placeholder MAC that didn't match the actual robot. Claude helped me reformat the MAC address 58:8C:81:AD:95:50 into the correct C++ byte array syntax: {0x58, 0x8C, 0x81, 0xAD, 0x95, 0x50}. --- I also had trouble finding enough power. My batteries were 9 volt, which was the largest and most powerful I could fit in my housing (after multiple iterations). A battery lost power and it was enough of a difference to not power the XIAO anymore and only the stepper motors, so then the robot didn't receive any input from the controller.
